Constant mean curvature surfaces

Constant mean curvature surfaces (CMC surfaces for short) in $\mathbb{R}^3$ can be locally obtained via DPW method. For the details, we kindly refer the readers to WISKY note and Ogata-Yasumoto(2017) for examples. In the DPW algorithm, we can construct CMC surfaces from a holomorphic potential, of the form \[ \xi = \sum_{j=-1}^{\infty} \xi_j(z) \lambda^j dz, \] where $\lambda \in \mathbb{S}^1 \subset \mathbb{C}$ and the $\xi_j(z)$ are $2 \times 2$ matrices the are independent of $\lambda$ and are holomorphic functions of a coordinate $z$ of some Riemann surface $\Sigma$. Furtheremore, the $\xi_j(z)$ are traceless and diagonal (resp. off-diagonal) when $j$ is even (resp. odd). Therefore, we just write the holomorphic potential to obtain each surface in the followings.
